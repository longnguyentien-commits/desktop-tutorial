# 00. Game Overview

## Concept

**Dragon vs Monsters: Hatchery Defense** is a 2D lane-based tactical defense game. The player recruits dragons from a TFT-style shop, places them onto a 5-lane battlefield, merges duplicates into stronger star levels, and protects a Dragon Egg from monsters advancing from right to left.

The game combines:

- **Plants vs. Zombies-style defense:** readable lanes, incoming waves, monsters moving toward the player base.
- **Teamfight Tactics-style drafting:** 5-slot shop, reroll, lock shop, bench, XP, board cap, and 3-copy merging.
- **Element synergies:** dragons activate traits at 2/4/6/8 unique on-board stacks.

## Pillars

1. **Readable Lane Defense**
   Monsters move from right to left across 5 lanes. Dragons face right and fire toward incoming enemies.

2. **TFT Economy And Merging**
   The player buys dragons, manages a 10-slot bench, rerolls or locks the shop, buys Keeper XP, and merges 3 copies into a higher-star dragon.

3. **Element Identity**
   The 8 elements have distinct combat effects and projectile visuals: Fire, Water, Ice, Wind, Plant, Earth, Energy, and Metal.

4. **High-Pressure Base Defense**
   The Dragon Egg has 10 HP. Every enemy that reaches the breach line removes 1 HP. At 0 HP, the game stops and displays **You Lost**.

5. **Short Tactical Runs**
   The current run structure is 20 waves with increasing enemy density and dedicated boss waves on wave 10 and wave 20.

## Target Audience

- Players who enjoy Plants vs. Zombies, Teamfight Tactics, Kingdom Rush, and light roguelike autobattlers.
- PC-first prototype, with controls designed around mouse drag/drop and right-click tooltips.
- Session target: 10-20 minutes.

## Current Presentation

- HTML5 canvas game view.
- 5 horizontal lanes, 9 columns.
- Field uses `Resource Asset/Map đấu.png`.
- Dragon Egg objective uses `Resource Asset/Tower.png`.
- Dragon Egg damage state is visible through a 10-segment HP bar, smoke below 50% HP, and fire below 20% HP.
- Dragons use Asset DML sprites:
  - 1-star uses Baby asset.
  - 2-star uses Adult asset.
  - 3-star uses Adult asset with a silhouette-following edge glow.
- Monsters use animated GIFs when available.
- Portals are generated by canvas when monsters spawn, then fade out. Normal monsters use lane portals; boss spawns use one large portal.

## Core Loop

1. Buy dragons from the shop.
2. Drag dragons to the bench or board, or click a shop dragon to buy it into the bench.
3. Reroll or lock the shop.
4. Buy XP to increase Keeper Level and board cap.
5. Merge duplicates into 2-star and 3-star dragons.
6. Start the wave manually, or let the 30-second planning timer auto-start from wave 2 onward.
7. Dragons attack monsters automatically.
8. Monsters attack dragons if blocked or in range.
9. Defeated waves grant Gold, 2 free XP, and a free shop reroll unless the shop is locked.
10. Survive through wave 20 and defeat the final Big Bad Boss to win.

Current control note: **Reroll** keeps the yellow button style, **Buy XP** uses the purple tier-4 style, and **Speed Up** cycles through Speed Up, Speed 2x, and Speed 3x.

## Win And Lose

- **Win:** Defeat the final wave 20 Big Bad Boss encounter. The screen displays **You Won** in green.
- **Lose:** Dragon Egg HP reaches 0. The screen displays **You Lost**, stops all game actions, and shows **Play Again**.

## Current Vertical Slice Goals

- Make the shop, bench, drag/drop, sell zone, merge, and level systems feel like a compact TFT layer.
- Make combat readable with distinct elemental projectiles.
- Make the Dragon Egg health state obvious with HP segments, damage numbers, smoke, and fire.
- Validate wave pacing, economy, and board-cap progression across 20 waves.
